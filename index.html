Eastern · 掌相

Chinese Palmistry · 三千年的觀察

Chinese palmistry dates back over three thousand years, rooted in the Taoist belief that the body reflects the patterns of Heaven and Earth. Practitioners studied the lines, mounts, and contours of the hand not as fixed destiny, but as a living map that shifts as the person grows. The hand was understood as a record of character, of tendency, of the accumulated weight of how a person moves through the world. A diagnostic tool used by physicians, philosophers, and advisors to emperors alike.

Western · Tarot

Tarot · A Mirror Across Centuries

Tarot emerged in fifteenth-century Europe as a card game before becoming a tool of reflection in the eighteenth century. Adopted by mystics, psychologists, and philosophers, its 78 cards were recognised not as magic but as a remarkably complete map of human experience: joy, loss, ambition, fear, transition, renewal. Carl Jung saw in tarot the architecture of the collective unconscious, universal patterns that surface differently in every individual life. The cards do not predict. They name what is already present.

Where They Meet

"Developed independently, across different continents and centuries, both systems arrived at the same observation: that human behaviour, tendency, and inner conflict follow recognisable patterns. Separated by three thousand miles and three thousand years. The same map. The convergence is not mysticism. It is evidence."
